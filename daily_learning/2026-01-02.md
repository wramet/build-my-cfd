## 1. Theory and Design Decisions

### 1.1 Mathematical Foundation

#### Linear Systems in CFD

The finite volume discretization of the Navier-Stokes equations leads to large sparse linear systems of the form:

$$
\mathbf{A} \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}
$$

Where:
- $\mathbf{A} \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times N}$ is the sparse coefficient matrix
- $\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^{N}$ is the solution vector (e.g., pressure, velocity components)
- $\mathbf{b} \in \mathbb{R}^{N}$ is the right-hand side vector
- $N$ is the number of cells in the mesh

For pressure-velocity coupling in incompressible/low-Mach flows, the pressure equation is derived from the continuity equation:

$$
\nabla \cdot \mathbf{U} = S_{\text{expansion}}
$$

Where $S_{\text{expansion}}$ represents the **Expansion Term** due to phase change (evaporation/condensation). This is critical for two-phase flows:

$$
S_{\text{expansion}} = \frac{\dot{m}'''}{\rho_l - \rho_v} \left( \frac{1}{\rho_v} - \frac{1}{\rho_l} \right)
$$

This term makes the pressure equation **non-homogeneous** (∇·U ≠ 0), affecting solver convergence.

#### Iterative Solvers

Direct solvers (LU, QR) are prohibitively expensive for large CFD problems ($O(N^3)$ complexity). Iterative methods are preferred:

**1. Conjugate Gradient (CG)**

For symmetric positive-definite matrices (e.g., pressure Poisson equation):

$$
\mathbf{r}_0 = \mathbf{b} - \mathbf{A}\mathbf{x}_0, \quad \mathbf{p}_0 = \mathbf{r}_0
$$

$$
\alpha_k = \frac{\mathbf{r}_k^T \mathbf{r}_k}{\mathbf{p}_k^T \mathbf{A} \mathbf{p}_k}, \quad
\mathbf{x}_{k+1} = \mathbf{x}_k + \alpha_k \mathbf{p}_k
$$

$$
\mathbf{r}_{k+1} = \mathbf{r}_k - \alpha_k \mathbf{A} \mathbf{p}_k, \quad
\beta_k = \frac{\mathbf{r}_{k+1}^T \mathbf{r}_{k+1}}{\mathbf{r}_k^T \mathbf{r}_k}, \quad
\mathbf{p}_{k+1} = \mathbf{r}_{k+1} + \beta_k \mathbf{p}_k
$$

**2. GMRES (Generalized Minimal Residual)**

For non-symmetric matrices (e.g., momentum equations with convection):

Minimizes $\|\mathbf{b} - \mathbf{A}\mathbf{x}\|_2$ over the Krylov subspace $\mathcal{K}_k(\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{r}_0)$. Requires restarts to limit memory usage.

**3. BiCGStab (Biconjugate Gradient Stabilized)**

For non-symmetric systems with faster convergence than GMRES for some CFD problems:

$$
\mathbf{r}_k^* = \mathbf{r}_0 \quad \text{(shadow residual)}
$$

Combines BiCG steps with steepest descent steps for stability.

#### Preconditioning

Preconditioning transforms the system to improve spectral properties:

$$
\mathbf{M}^{-1} \mathbf{A} \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{M}^{-1} \mathbf{b}
$$

**Common Preconditioners:**

1. **Jacobi (Diagonal)**: $\mathbf{M} = \text{diag}(\mathbf{A})$
   - Simple, parallel, but ineffective for ill-conditioned systems

2. **Gauss-Seidel**: $\mathbf{M} = \mathbf{D} + \mathbf{L}$ (lower triangular)
   - Better convergence, sequential by nature

3. **Incomplete LU (ILU)**: $\mathbf{A} \approx \mathbf{L}\mathbf{U}$
   - ILU(0): same sparsity pattern as $\mathbf{A}$
   - Fill-in variants (ILU(k)) improve convergence at higher cost

4. **Algebraic Multigrid (AMG)**:
   - Optimal $O(N)$ complexity for elliptic problems
   - Essential for large-scale CFD simulations

#### Convergence Criteria

Typical stopping criteria for CFD solvers:

$$
\frac{\|\mathbf{r}_k\|_2}{\|\mathbf{r}_0\|_2} < \epsilon_{\text{rel}}, \quad
\|\mathbf{r}_k\|_2 < \epsilon_{\text{abs}}, \quad
k < k_{\text{max}}
$$

Typical values: $\epsilon_{\text{rel}} = 10^{-6}$ to $10^{-8}$

### 1.2 Design Decisions

#### Why Iterative Solvers in CFD?

1. **Memory Efficiency**: Sparse storage ($O(N)$) vs dense ($O(N^2)$)
2. **Scalability**: Matrix-free implementations possible
3. **Flexibility**: Easy to swap solvers/preconditioners
4. **Physical Accuracy**: Can leverage problem structure (e.g., pressure Poisson)

#### Trade-offs

| Aspect | Direct Methods | Iterative Methods |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| Accuracy | Exact (up to round-off) | Approximate (tolerance-dependent) |
| Time | $O(N^3)$ | $O(N \cdot \sqrt{\kappa(\mathbf{A})})$ per iteration |
| Memory | $O(N^2)$ | $O(N)$ |
| Parallelism | Limited | Excellent |
| Robustness | High | Problem-dependent |

**Preconditioner Trade-offs:**
- **Jacobi**: Fast per iteration, slow convergence
- **ILU**: Good convergence, memory overhead, less parallel
- **AMG**: Best for elliptic, complex to implement

#### Common PITFALLS

1. **Poor Diagonal Dominance**
   - **Symptom**: Solver stagnation or divergence
   - **Cause**: Inappropriate discretization, high aspect ratio cells
   - **Fix**: Under-relaxation, better mesh quality, preconditioning

2. **Incorrect Boundary Conditions**
   - **Symptom**: Wrong solution near boundaries, slow convergence
   - **Cause**: Inconsistent treatment in matrix assembly
   - **Fix**: Strong BC enforcement (modify matrix rows)

3. **Expansion Term Neglect**
   - **Symptom**: Mass imbalance in phase-change flows
   - **Cause**: Assuming ∇·U = 0 for evaporating flows
   - **Fix**: Include $S_{\text{expansion}}$ in pressure equation RHS

4. **Turbulence Effects (Re > 2300)**
   - **Symptom**: Unphysical oscillations, wrong separation prediction
   - **Cause**: Laminar solver in turbulent regime
   - **Fix**: Use turbulence model (k-ε, k-ω, LES), adjust solver tolerance

5. **Density Ratio Issues**
   - **Symptom**: Pressure oscillations at interface
   - **Cause**: Large $\rho_l/\rho_v$ ratio (up to 1000:1 for water/steam)
   - **Fix**: Rhie-Chow interpolation, implicit treatment

#### What YOUR Engine Needs

1. **Flexible Solver Interface**
   - Abstract base class for solvers
   - Runtime selection of solver/preconditioner combination
   - Configurable tolerances and max iterations

2. **Sparse Matrix Storage**
   - CSR format for matrix-vector operations
   - Efficient assembly from finite volume discretization
   - Support for block-structured matrices (velocity-pressure coupling)

3. **Preconditioner Library**
   - Start with Jacobi (baseline)
   - Add ILU(0) for moderate problems
   - Integrate AMG for production (e.g., hypre, PETSc)

4. **Phase-Change Support**
   - Expansion source term in pressure equation
   - Implicit treatment of evaporation mass transfer
   - Special handling for large density ratios

5. **Convergence Monitoring**
   - Residual history logging
   - Divergence detection and recovery
   - Adaptive tolerance based on outer iteration

### 1.3 Key Concepts

#### Important Terms

- **Krylov Subspace**: $\mathcal{K}_k(\mathbf{A}, \mathbf{r}_0) = \text{span}\{\mathbf{r}_0, \mathbf{A}\mathbf{r}_0, \mathbf{A}^2\mathbf{r}_0, \ldots, \mathbf{A}^{k-1}\mathbf{r}_0\}$
- **Condition Number**: $\kappa(\mathbf{A}) = \|\mathbf{A}\| \cdot \|\mathbf{A}^{-1}\|$, measures sensitivity to perturbations
- **Spectral Radius**: $\rho(\mathbf{A}) = \max_i |\lambda_i|$, determines asymptotic convergence rate
- **Preconditioner**: Matrix $\mathbf{M}$ approximating $\mathbf{A}$ such that $\mathbf{M}^{-1}\mathbf{A}$ has better spectral properties
- **Fill-in**: Non-zero entries in $\mathbf{L}$ or $\mathbf{U}$ factors where $\mathbf{A}$ was zero (ILU factorization)

#### Physical Interpretation

- **Pressure Equation**: Enforces mass conservation, modified by phase change
- **Momentum Equation**: Convection-diffusion-reaction system, non-symmetric due to convection
- **Expansion Term**: Represents volume generation/loss from phase change (evaporation adds mass to vapor phase)
- **Turbulence**: Adds Reynolds stresses to momentum equations, increases effective viscosity, affects conditioning

#### Warning Signs of Wrong Implementation

| Symptom | Possible Cause | Fix |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Residual oscillates | Wrong preconditioner, ill-conditioned matrix | Try AMG, check mesh quality |
| Slow convergence | Poor initial guess, tight tolerance | Use previous solution as guess, relax tolerance |
| Divergence | Negative diagonal, unstable discretization | Check time step, under-relaxation |
| Wrong pressure field | Missing expansion term, incorrect BC | Add $S_{\text{expansion}}$, verify BC implementation |
| Mass imbalance | Inconsistent velocity-pressure coupling | Check Rhie-Chow interpolation |
| Interface smearing | Wrong HTC calculation, density ratio issues | Verify heat transfer coefficient, use sharpening |
| Unphysical separation | Laminar assumption in turbulent flow | Add turbulence model (Re > 2300) |

#### Solver Selection Guide

- **Pressure (Poisson-type)**: CG + AMG (symmetric, elliptic)
- **Momentum (Convection-diffusion)**: GMRES/BiCGStab + ILU (non-symmetric)
- **Energy (Advection-diffusion)**: BiCGStab + Jacobi/GS
- **Two-phase with phase change**: GMRES + AMG with expansion term treatment
